Rajasthan, the "Land of Kings," is a vibrant state in northwestern India known for its royal heritage. It boasts majestic forts, opulent palaces, and colorful festivals that reflect its rich history. From the golden deserts of Jaisalmer to the lakes of Udaipur, its landscapes are diverse and stunning. Rajasthan offers a blend of cultural traditions, folk music, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality.

  • Jaisalmer – The “Golden City,” with its sandstone fort, desert safaris, and havelis.
    •     
  • Jodhpur –The “Blue City,” home to the mighty Mehrangarh Fort and Umaid Bhawan Palace..
    •     
  • Pushkar – A holy town known for the Brahma Temple and its annual camel fair.
    •     
  • Mount Abu – Rajasthan’s only hill station, with cool weather and the Dilwara Temples.
    •     
  • Ranthambore National Park – A top wildlife destination for spotting tigers and other wildlife.
    •     
  • Chittorgarh –Famous for its massive fort, one of the largest in India, steeped in Rajput history.
    •     
  • Bikaner–Known for Junagarh Fort, Karni Mata Temple (rat temple), and camel breeding.
    •     
  • Ajmer – A pilgrimage center known for the Ajmer Sharif Dargah and scenic Ana Sagar Lake.

Religions in India

India is often called the land of spirituality because it is the birthplace of several of the world’s major religions and home to many others. Here, faith is not just a personal belief but an integral part of daily life, influencing festivals, traditions, art, and social values. The country’s religious diversity reflects its philosophy of “unity in diversity”, where multiple faiths coexist and shape its cultural identity.


Hinduism


The oldest religion in India, Hinduism is followed by the majority of the population. It centers around a variety of gods and goddesses, sacred texts like the Vedas and Bhagavad Gita, and practices such as yoga, meditation, and temple worship. Festivals like Diwali, Holi, and Navratri are rooted in Hindu traditions.

christianity


Christianity came to India as early as the 1st century CE, with St. Thomas the Apostle believed to have landed in Kerala. Today, churches like the Basilica of Bom Jesus in Goa and St. Thomas Cathedral in Chennai are important heritage sites.

islam


Introduced to India in the 7th century, Islam grew under the Delhi Sultanate and the Mughal Empire. It is based on the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad in the Qur’an. Festivals like Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha are celebrated across the country.

Colors, Lights, and Rituals: Indian Festivals Through the Year

India is a land of diverse cultures and religions, and this diversity is beautifully reflected in its vibrant festivals. Each festival tells a story—of faith, harvest, history, or community—and is celebrated with joy, colors, music, and rituals.
FESTIVALS DESCRIPTION IMAGE
Diwali Diwali is the Hindu festival of lights, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.
Holi The festival of colors, celebrating the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil.
Nava rathri Celebrates the triumph of Lord Rama over Ravana, symbolizing the victory of good over evil.
Ganesh chathurthi Marks the birth of Lord Ganesha, celebrated with devotion, prayers, and idol immersion.
Raksha bhandhan A festival where sisters tie a rakhi on their brothers’ wrists, symbolizing love and protection.
Sankranthi A harvest festival celebrated across India, marking the sun’s transition into Capricorn and the arrival of longer days.
Ugadhi The Telugu and Kannada New Year festival, celebrating new beginnings with prayers, feasts, and traditional rituals.
